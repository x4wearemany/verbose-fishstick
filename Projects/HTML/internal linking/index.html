Introduction

This is a brief article on how to set up a Proxmox Virtual Environment

Prerequisites

Before we get started here there are a few things we recommend you have ready

  • A computer/server machine capable of virtualization
  • A USB Thumb drive
  • A Laptop/PC to make a "bootable" USB stick

Setup

To get started you'll need to go to the Proxmox website and download the latest version of Proxmox. While you're at it download your selected thumb drive flasher and open it.
Once opened insert your USB thumb drive into your computer and select the Proxmox ISO image and start the flash.
When it finishes unplug your thumb drive and insert it into the computer you'll be using to make your Virtual Environment and turn it on booting into the boot menu and select the USB thumb drive.
There may be additional setup required in your system BIOS to get your virtualization working, We do not cover that in this article.

Installation and Your first VM

Once in the install screen its pretty self explanatory, go through the graphical installation and while you go through if you have a FQDN don't forget to assign that in the correct screen. (i did my first time)
If you don't have a FQDN feel free to leave it as default, and remember to set the ip as something that isn't already used. Also set your password as something memorable

Once install is complete it will reboot and go into its "terminal" and will show you the ip and port to put in to get to the local web interface. It will look something like this "https://192.168.0.20:8006"
Paste that into your browser and you'll be at the local web interface, once there input your username (root) and your chosen password (in the Realm it is Linux PAM NOT Proxmox VE) When you're finally logged in we recommend you set up a ZFS Share, to do so you will click your Node and click the disks drop down menu and click ZFS, click Create: ZFS and select the disk(s) you want in that partition

Next you will need to upload another ISO image to your server and that is super easy. In the far left with the drop down menu for your node you'll see something labeled "local (node)" click on that
There will be another vertical menu with 5 options, click on "ISO Images" with the disk icon, click upload and select file. Select the ISO you want to use and upload it by hitting the "open" button

Once that is uploaded you can make your first VM! To make one all you have to do is click the blue button in the top right corner of the screen that's labeled "Create VM" with a monitor icon
Put in the name of the VM, i'll go with "Lorem-ipsum" for shits and giggles. Click next and select the drop down ISO image menu and select the ISO you want to use (i recommend Ubuntu server) and click next
For the system tab i don't recommend you adjust anything so just click next again. For the tutorial we recommend you stick with 32GB, just make sure it's on the correct storage you made
For the CPU tab it depends on your system, you can find the amount of CPUs you have in the node summary as well as the amount of ram you have. For mine i have a 2 sockets with 12 cores per CPU. For the tutorial we will go with 1 Socket and 6 cores. In the memory tab you can leave it at the default for our use-case, click next and ignore the network tab for now and click next again. The Confirm tab is an overview of your settings just so you can double check them before creating the VM. If all looks in order click the "Finish" button and it'll create the VM
All that is left to do is to start your VM, click on the newly created vm and select the "Console" tab, click the power button on the left hand side and select "Start" and your VM will start right up

CONGRATULATIONS!!! You just created your very first VM! Thank you for reading and following this article and have a blessed day.
